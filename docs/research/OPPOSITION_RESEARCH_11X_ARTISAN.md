# Opposition Research Report: 11x.ai & Artisan AI

**Purpose:** Learn from competitors' mistakes to avoid replicating them in Agency OS  
**Date:** January 7, 2026  
**Sources:** G2, TechCrunch, Trustpilot, Glassdoor, Reddit, Medium, industry analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Both 11x.ai and Artisan AI represent cautionary tales in the AI SDR space. Despite raising significant VC funding ($74M+ for 11x, $12M+ for Artisan), both companies face serious criticism around:

1. **Product-market fit issues** — Products don't deliver promised results
2. **Deceptive practices** — Misleading customers and investors about capabilities
3. **Toxic workplace cultures** — Leading to high employee turnover
4. **Predatory contracts** — Locking customers into annual agreements with no exit
5. **Poor customer outcomes** — High churn, zero results after months of use

**Key Lesson:** Don't raise more money than your product can justify. Deliver real value before scaling sales.

## 11x.ai Deep Dive

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2022 (London, moved to SF)
- **Funding:** $74M+ (Benchmark Series A, a16z $50M Series B)
- **Product:** AI SDR "Alice" for automated cold outreach
- **Pricing:** ~$5,000/month (annual contracts required)

### The Scandal (March 2025)

TechCrunch published a devastating exposé revealing:

#### 1. Fake Customer Claims
- **ZoomInfo** publicly stated they were NEVER a customer, only did a 1-month trial where 11x "performed significantly worse than our SDR employees"
- 11x continued claiming ZoomInfo as a customer for 12+ months after they churned
- ZoomInfo threatened legal action for trademark infringement
- **Airtable** also denied being a customer — only did a "very short trial" that "never rolled out to the sales team"
- Multiple other companies' logos used without permission

#### 2. Inflated Revenue Metrics
- Used "Contracted ARR (CARR)" counting 3-month trial customers as annual contracts
- Internal sources say "They absolutely massaged the numbers internally when it came to growth and churn"
- Claimed ~$10M ARR but actual sustainable revenue reportedly much lower
- Investors realized they paid 116x revenue instead of 35x they thought

#### 3. Catastrophic Churn Rates
- **70-80% of customers churned** after their initial period
- Internal Slack messages showed retention at only 20-30%
- One Trustpilot reviewer: "0 meetings booked in 6 months"
- Medium reviewer: "$2,500 cost per positive reply" — astronomical CAC

#### 4. Product Doesn't Work
- "The products barely work" — former engineer
- AI hallucinations went unchecked to customers
- Customers had to manually check and correct AI work, "defeating the purpose"
- Emails sent to wrong customers, causing immediate contract terminations
- No reply handling — requires separate staff to respond

### Workplace Culture Issues (Glassdoor/Sifted)

- 60+ hour work weeks standard
- CEO warned: "if you are not willing to push hard...you don't deserve to be at 11x"
- Public call-outs over Slack
- Workers sleeping overnight in the office
- "Capricious leadership: best buds when things go well"
- High turnover — "most of the pros have already churned"
- CRO told candidate "working at 11x meant you won't have a life"
- "Heavy bro vibes" and lack of diversity
- Salary payment concerns for departing employees

### Contract Practices
- Annual contracts with 3-month opt-out periods
- Users report being unable to exercise their break clauses
- "Vendor locking" — 11x owns the domains they set up for you
- "They were resistant to signing any sort of trial"

## Common Failure Patterns

### 1. Overpromising, Underdelivering
Both companies sell "AI SDR that replaces humans" but deliver automation that requires constant babysitting.

**Agency OS Lesson:** 
- Be honest about what AI can and can't do
- Position as "AI-augmented" not "AI replacement"
- Under-promise, over-deliver

### 2. Premature Scaling
Both raised large rounds before product-market fit, then had to maintain growth metrics that weren't sustainable.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- Prove retention before scaling sales
- Don't raise more than you can justify with real metrics
- Focus on 20 founding customers before broader launch

### 3. Vanity Metrics Over Real Value
Both tracked "contracted ARR" and customer logos instead of actual retained revenue and customer success.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- Track: Meetings booked, replies generated, actual revenue impact
- Measure 90-day retention as primary metric
- Only count customers who've renewed once

### 4. No Reply Handling
Neither platform handles replies, which is critical for cold outreach success.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- ✅ Already planned: Intent classification, reply routing
- Make reply handling a core feature, not an afterthought

### 5. Generic Personalization
Both scrape basic info and inject it into templates — recipients can tell immediately it's AI.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- Deep Research capability for Hot leads (ALS > 85)
- Multi-layer personalization: Industry pain points + Company specifics + Individual signals
- Quality over quantity

### 6. Predatory Contracts
Annual contracts with difficult exits destroy trust and generate negative word-of-mouth.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- Offer monthly contracts (premium) and quarterly (standard)
- Easy cancellation process
- "Founding tier" creates goodwill without contractual lock-in

### 7. Toxic Culture = Bad Product
High turnover and burnout lead to buggy products and poor customer experience.

**Agency OS Lesson:**
- Sustainable pace from day one
- As a solo founder + AI, avoid the culture toxicity entirely
- Hire first engineer at month 3 (per financial plan)

## Actionable Takeaways for Agency OS

### Pre-Launch
1. ✅ Complete Phase 21 E2E testing — don't launch buggy
2. ✅ Ensure scraper waterfall handles Cloudflare sites
3. ✅ Test real email campaigns during warmup period
4. ✅ Document honest expected results (don't oversell)

### Launch Strategy
1. 20 founding customers only — prove retention first
2. Monthly contracts available from day one
3. Transparent pricing on website
4. Real case studies with actual metrics before scaling

### Product Priorities
1. Reply handling must work flawlessly
2. Deep Research personalization for Hot leads
3. Clear quality controls on AI-generated content
4. Dashboard shows real metrics, not vanity stats

### Culture
1. Solo founder + AI = no toxic startup culture
2. First hire at month 3, not month 19
3. Sustainable pace — Agency OS can't burn out

### Metrics to Track
1. **Primary:** 90-day customer retention rate
2. **Secondary:** Meetings booked per customer per month
3. **Tertiary:** Reply rate, positive reply rate
4. **Never:** "Contracted ARR" or trial customers as retained

## Conclusion

11x and Artisan prove that VC funding and slick marketing can't compensate for a product that doesn't work. Both companies:

- Raised too much money too fast
- Shipped before the product was ready
- Made promises they couldn't keep
- Created toxic cultures that produced bad products
- Locked customers into contracts to hide churn
- May face legal consequences (11x already has legal threats)

**Agency OS has the opportunity to be the "anti-11x":**
- Australian focus = defensible niche
- Multi-channel from day one (not just email)
- Real personalization through Deep Research
- Honest metrics and transparent pricing
- No predatory contracts
- Built by a solo founder who cares about quality

**The bar is low. Don't just clear it — set a new standard.**
